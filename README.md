Here’s a clear overview of the Student Performance Dataset:

General Structure:
Fields included: Student_ID, Student_Name, Gender, Department, Semester, Subject_1–4 scores, Attendance, Marks, Sum, Final_Result.

Total records: 140 (IDs 101–140) + 40 (IDs 201–240) = 180 students.

Performance Insights:

Pass vs Fail:
Majority of students have a Pass result.
Failures are scattered across departments, especially in BCA and some BA/BSc entries.

Top Performers:
Highest total scores are around 738 (Sneha Patel, BBA, Semester 5).
Several students in BCA and BBA consistently score above 690.

Lowest Performers:
Scores below 400 (e.g., Anil – BCA, Semester 3 with 387).
Failures often correlate with low subject averages and attendance below ~60.

Departmental Trends:
BCA: Mixed performance. Some of the highest scores (Shreya, Lakshmi) but also many failures (Arjun, Anil, Varun, Suresh, Harish, Vinay).
BBA: Generally strong, with multiple students scoring above 690 (Priya, Rani, Sneha Patel).
BCom: Mostly average performers, fewer extreme highs or lows.
BA & BSc: More variability. Some very high scores (Priya Verma – BA, 709; Nikhil Das – BSc, 729) but also frequent failures.

Key Observations:
Attendance seems linked to success: Students with attendance above 85% usually pass and score higher.
Semester effect: Higher semesters (5–6) show more failures, possibly due to tougher subjects.
Gender distribution: Both male and female students are represented across departments, with no clear performance bias.
